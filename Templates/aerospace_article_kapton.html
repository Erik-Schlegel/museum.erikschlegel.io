Apollo 11 Command Module Protective Foil Sample

"Kapton" Foil

  • Kapton Foil is a semi-transparent, gold-colored, aluminized polyimide film.
  • Multiple layers of Kapton foil covered the CSM (aka Columbia) and LEM (aka Eagle) crafts.
  • Protected Cmdr. Neil Armstrong, LEM Pilot Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin, and CSM Pilot Michael Collins from temperature extremes during the Apollo 11 Mission.

Saturn V rocket takeoff from Cape Kennedy at 9:32 AM on July 16, 1969. Saturn V is the largest and most powerful rocket ever launched.

Armstrong and Aldrin land the LEM on the moon at 4:17 PM on July 20, 1969.

While Armstrong and Aldrin explored the moon, Collins pilots the CSM around the moon 25 times. Orbiting at an altitude of 61.6 miles.
